Your February Oklahoma Lawn Care Guide

Hello Oklahoma City homeowner!

February might still feel like winter, but it's the perfect time to prepare your lawn for a spectacular spring. Here are your essential tasks for this month:

1. Soil Testing & Amendment

Why: Oklahoma's clay-heavy soil needs proper pH balance (6.0-7.0) for optimal grass growth.

Action: Test your soil now. If pH is low, apply lime. If high, add sulfur. Most OKC lawns benefit from 50 lbs of lime per 1,000 sq ft.

2. Pre-Emergent Herbicide Application

Why: Apply before soil reaches 55¬∞F (usually mid-February in OKC) to prevent crabgrass and other weeds.

Action: Use prodiamine or dithiopyr. Apply evenly across lawn, focusing on edges and bare spots.

3. Dormant Seeding for Thin Areas

Why: February freezes/thaws help work seed into soil for better germination in spring.

Action: Overseed thin areas with Bermuda or Fescue (depending on your lawn type). Lightly rake to ensure seed-to-soil contact.
