Welcome to Your February Lawn Care Guide!

Hello Oklahoma City homeowners! As we navigate the transition from winter to early spring, February presents unique opportunities to prepare your lawn for the growing season ahead. Oklahoma's unpredictable weather patterns mean now is the perfect time to get ahead of common lawn issues.

In this edition, we'll cover essential February lawn care tasks, recommend trusted local services, and provide DIY tips to keep your lawn healthy and vibrant.

1. Soil Testing & Analysis

According to Oklahoma State University Extension, February is the ideal time for soil testing. Oklahoma soils often need pH adjustments and specific nutrient balancing. Take 10-15 soil cores from your lawn (3-4 inches deep) and bring them to your local County Extension office for analysis. Proper pH (6.0-6.5) ensures all essential nutrients are available for optimal turf growth.

2. Winter Weed Control

February is prime time for post-emergence winter weed control. Target weeds like annual bluegrass, chickweed, and henbit while they're young and actively growing. For bermudagrass lawns, consider applying Roundup when the grass is fully dormant (usually January or early February) for effective control.

3. Thatch Removal Preparation

Late February is the perfect time to prepare for thatch removal on warm-season lawns (bermudagrass and zoysiagrass). Check your thatch layer - if it's thicker than 0.5 inches, plan to dethatch just before spring green-up. Excessive thatch impedes water, nutrient, and air movement into the soil.

4. Equipment Maintenance

Sharpen mower blades and service your lawn equipment now. Dull blades bruise grass tips and create a dull appearance. Properly adjusted equipment ensures clean cuts and healthier grass. Also, check irrigation systems for winter damage and prepare for spring activation.

5. Plan Your Fertilization Schedule

Based on OSU recommendations, bermudagrass lawns need about 5 pounds of nitrogen per 1000 sq ft annually. Plan your applications: spring complete fertilizer, summer slow-release applications, and fall fertilization. Cool-season grasses like tall fescue need most fertilizer in fall with minimal summer applications.

Proper Mowing Techniques for Oklahoma Lawns

Bermudagrass: Cut at 0.5-0.75 inches May-August, raise to 1.0-1.25 inches September-April

Tall Fescue: Maintain at 3.0 inches during summer, 2.5 inches in cooler months

Pro Tip: Never remove more than ‚Öì of leaf height at one mowing. Use sharp blades and vary mowing patterns to reduce soil compaction.

Smart Watering Practices

Oklahoma's clay soils require deep, infrequent watering. Water only when grass shows first signs of wilt (footprinting, blue-gray color). Apply enough water to moisten soil to 6-inch depth. Early morning watering reduces evaporation and disease risk.

Don't Bag Your Clippings!

OSU Extension recommends leaving grass clippings on the lawn. They decompose quickly, returning valuable nutrients (especially nitrogen) to the soil. This practice saves time, reduces landfill waste, and feeds your lawn naturally.
