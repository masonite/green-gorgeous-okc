Soil Testing & Amendment

Why: Oklahoma's clay-heavy soil needs proper pH balance (6.0-7.0) for optimal grass growth.

Action: Test your soil now. Free soil test kits available at OSU Extension Office.

Aeration Timing

When: Soil is moist but not soggy (late February - early March).

Why: Reduces compaction from winter, improves water and nutrient absorption.

Pre-Emergent Herbicide

Critical Timing: Apply before soil reaches 55°F (usually early March in OKC).

Target: Prevents crabgrass and other weeds. Product recommendation: Prodiamine.

First Mowing Guidelines

Height: Set mower to 3-3.5 inches for fescue/bermuda.

Tip: Never remove more than ⅓ of grass blade length to avoid stress.

Reader Q&A

"When should I fertilize my lawn in Oklahoma?"

Answer: For cool-season grasses (fescue), apply fertilizer in early March. For warm-season grasses (bermuda), wait until late April when soil temperatures reach 65°F. Use a slow-release nitrogen fertilizer (1 lb per 1000 sq ft).

"How often should I water in spring?"

Answer: In March-April, lawns need about 1 inch of water per week. Water deeply but infrequently to encourage deep root growth. Early morning watering reduces evaporation and disease risk.
